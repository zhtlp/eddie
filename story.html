My Story: New Zealand Grit

From a Break to a Breakthrough

July 15, 2018. I broke my right tibia and fibula on the court. In the ambulance, I cried: “I’m sorry, Mom. We can’t go to New Zealand anymore.” Four hours later, I woke up to an external fixator on my leg—and a fear that the life of sports I loved had ended. It hadn’t. It was just changing.

The Fall

As I was dribbling and running, someone much older hit me. My right leg snapped—tibia and fibula fractured. For weeks, I dreamt I was back on the court, running, being knocked down, breaking my leg. Being knocked down, and breaking my leg. Those dreams woke me again and again. I sat in a wheelchair, wondering if I would ever play sports again.

“I’m sorry, Mom. We can’t go to New Zealand anymore.”

The Turning Point

On September 29, 2018, the external fixator came off. Blood spurted from the screw holes—raw proof that healing is messy and real. Then Mom said: “On October 4, we’re going to New Zealand.”

“Impossible. How could I? On crutches, in a wheelchair… to New Zealand?”

And yet, on October 4, 2018, I boarded the flight to Auckland—on crutches. That choice didn’t just move me across 10,000 kilometers; it moved something inside me back into motion.

On Crutches, I Went Anyway

  • Mount EdenOn crutches, I climbed to the lookout.
  • HobbitonI stood at Bilbo Baggins’s door.
  • RotoruaI watched the haka—strength in rhythm.
  • Lake TekapoI saw the vast Milky Way.
  • QueenstownAt Kawarau Bridge Bungy, I promised: I’ll be back when I recover.
  • InvercargillThe southernmost city I had ever been.

Somewhere between fear and the next step, the nightmare let go. The world felt big again. And I felt ready to grow into it.

New Zealand—where a trip became a turning point.

Back to the Court

The nightmare never returned. I went back to the soccer field. I was still the key center on the basketball court. I fell—then stood back up, again and again. I grew stronger. Braver. I am no longer defined by the injury I once suffered, but by the strength I discovered through it.

With that strength, I am ready to face every challenge ahead.

Thank you for reading.

Not a story about loss, but about resolve—inch by inch, step by step, sometimes on crutches—choosing to move forward anyway.
